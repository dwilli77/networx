About Us

Meet the amazing team behind this project and find out more about how we work.

Background

Building relationships across social class lines is a complex process with many rewards and pitfalls. Best practices gleaned from other national initiatives, asset-based community development, strength-based assessment, a historical movement resulting in significant change, and other research-based material informed the development of the NETworX model.

Model and Strategy

At the model's core are weekly meetings that include shared meals, time for relationship-building and mutual accountability, goal setting, and celebration.  Childcare and transportation, frequent barriers to regular participation, are provided as needed without cost.  Education around the topics of self- awareness, racial equity, addiction in all of its forms, aspects of privilege, holistic poverty, social capital, the role of personal and community trauma, and community transformation form the topical base of the training classes.
Reciprocal relationships, built over a period, of time, are central and are built through the shared cohort experience and shared vulnerability.  Participants support one another as they develop their individual strengths, capacities, and potential. Finally, because the level of transformation desired takes place over time, participants are asked to dedicate 18-36 months to training and relationship building.

What does a NETworX Intentional Community Look Like?

A NETworX Intentional Community is created through cohorts that mirror the community at large. They should be diverse and inclusive of participants from various racial, economic, and social backgrounds invested in personal and community transformation through connection and igniting human potential.

Champion of Change

A hard working individual committed to moving out of poverty.

public

Community Ally

A middle class individual working with Champions to eliminate obstacles to their goals.

Champions of Change and Allies begin at a minimum with an interest in changing the mindset of poverty, having capacity for personal change, and committing to engaging openly with others, especially during the rough spots, toward a goal of personal and community well-being. For this reason participants share common criteria including (1) having a stable residence, (2) not actively abusing drugs or alcohol, (3) having a commitment to non-violent problem solving and (4) consistency/stability in addressing personal mental health needs.

Who we are

Annette Snider

Director of Operations

Annette works for RFDCDC as the NETworX USA Director of Operations. She has been involved with initiatives to end poverty since 2009. Annette is also a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist Associate and uses those systemic principles daily in her role with NETworX.

Annette Received her B.A. from Metropolitan State University, in Minneapolis, MN in Family Studies/Cultural Diversity Awareness.

She received her M.A. from Saint Mary’s University of Minnesota in Human Development with her Focus on Understanding Poverty.

Annette graduated from Pfeiffer University, Raleigh campus in the M.A. program for Marriage and Family Therapy and is a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist Associate (LMFTA).

Annette grew up in Minnesota, and loves living in North Carolina! Her hobbies include hiking, biking, kayaking, pretty much anything outdoors and in nature. Annette is passionate about travel and experiencing other cultures and foods. She gets anxious when she doesn’t have her next travel adventure in the works.

Annette describes herself as an extraverted introvert and a free thinker that needs reigning in occasionally. She really enjoys her own humor finding it quite hilarious and considers herself a storyteller. Annette admits she can be emotionally moved to tears by a good commercial.

One of her life goal is to write a book that someone not related to her actually reads.

Thomas Blake

Co-Director, NETworX USA Western Regional Center and Program Coordinator

Tom is from the Wilmington/ Wrightsville Beach area of NC, though he has spent most of his adult life elsewhere, including the mid-Atlantic, the mid-West, and now the West. He is an Episcopal priest, has served several congregations, and has been a deputy to the General Convention of the Episcopal Church three times, including service on the legislative committee on Social and Urban Affairs.

He was first exposed to a model of addressing poverty by bringing people together across class lines and building mutually supportive relationships while living in Muncie, IN. The congregation he served there provided office and program space for an organization that had successfully implemented such a model locally and had been on the forefront of it nationally. Having the opportunity to run a comparable organization and program in Carson City has been both enlightening and enriching. He has grown and continue to grow in amazing ways.

Rev. Dr. Rob Jennings-Teats

NETworX Western Regional Director

Rev. Dr. Rob Jennings-Teats currently lives with his partner in Carson City, Nevada and is an active participant in the local Carson City NETworX Affiliate. Lee Smedley

Volunteer Director of Support Connections

A native of West Virginia, Lee loves being outside, walking, hiking or bicycling. And, he loves singing too. He currently performs in a three-piece combo and as a member of his local church praise band. In the 1970’s, he performed as a strolling musician at Colonial Williamsburg, and has recorded a CD of his own original music.

An insufferable extrovert, Lee loves engaging others in conversations about possibilities. “You could even…..” is a phrase that elicits a groan from his son and wife, as he works to build on what someone has said, often far beyond where they have any interest in going. He recently started a men’s book club called Spirited Civil Discourse, where folks come together at a local restaurant to discuss current issues and ofttimes controversial books.
